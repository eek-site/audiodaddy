Privacy Policy

Last updated: 6 December 2024

The short version: We collect the minimum data needed to process your transcription. We don't sell your data. We delete your audio files after processing. No tracking cookies, no nonsense.

Who we are

Audio Daddy is operated by Road and Rescue Ltd, a company registered in England and Wales (Company No: 16401492). Our registered address is 71-75 Shelton Street, Covent Garden, London, WC2H 9JQ, United Kingdom.

What data we collect

Audio files

When you upload an audio file for transcription, we temporarily store it to process your request. Once transcription is complete, the audio file is deleted from our servers. We do not keep copies of your audio files.

Payment information

Payment processing is handled by Stripe. We do not store your card details on our servers. Stripe may collect and process:

  • Card number, expiry date, and CVC
  • Billing address
  • Email address (if provided)

For more information, see Stripe's Privacy Policy.

Contact form submissions

If you contact us through our website, we collect:

  • Your name
  • Email address
  • Message content

We use this information solely to respond to your enquiry.

Technical data

Our hosting provider (GitHub Pages) may collect standard server logs including IP addresses and browser information. We do not use analytics cookies or tracking scripts.

How we use your data

We use your data only to:

  • Process your transcription request
  • Process payment for the service
  • Respond to support enquiries
  • Comply with legal obligations

Data retention

  • Audio files: Deleted immediately after transcription
  • Transcripts: Not stored on our servers; delivered directly to you
  • Payment records: Retained as required by law (typically 6 years for tax purposes)
  • Contact enquiries: Retained for up to 2 years

Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Rectify inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Object to processing
  • Data portability
  • Lodge a complaint with the ICO
